# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Turkish university students sharing a flat with 2-5 roommates. They check the app casually on their phone, usually in the evening or right after a shared expense, to see what they owe or are owed. They are comfortable with mobile apps and expect the tool to stay out of their way; the social stakes are real (money between friends), so trust and clarity matter more than cleverness.

## Product Purpose

HouseTab tracks shared home expenses and auto-generates debts between roommates. Every expense creates a paper trail. Users can see who owes whom, mark payments, and let the app handle the math so the group avoids awkward conversations. Success means zero disputed balances and no "I thought you paid that" moments.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, professional, clean. The app handles real money between real friends. It earns trust by being precise and unambiguous, not by being warm or playful. Every number should feel authoritative. The UI gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

None specified. Design freely within the product register.

## Design Principles

1. **Numbers are the hero.** Amounts, totals, and balances are the product. Give them room; never shrink or de-emphasize them.
2. **Clarity over decoration.** If an element doesn't communicate something, it shouldn't exist. No flourishes that require explanation.
3. **Status is always visible.** Debt states (pending, paid, disputed) must be readable at a glance. Color alone is not enough; shape and label must reinforce it.
4. **Dark is deliberate.** The app uses a dark theme. This isn't aesthetic posturing: students check it in dim rooms, at night, on OLED screens. The dark palette reduces eye strain and battery use.
5. **Trust through precision.** Rounded numbers, vague labels, or clipped text break trust. Everything is exact.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Text and number contrast is non-negotiable. Respect reduced-motion preferences.
